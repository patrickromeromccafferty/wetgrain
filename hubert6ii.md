## Xì’ñú dón don <br>
<br>
He used to go to Xirágáá1 to exchange <br>
peaches for corn and beans <br>
to give as offerings at mass on Sunday <br>
and to conjure the spirits that would allow <br>
his son to descend from the Sierra <br>
or at least light up the way <br>
so he would find the way home. <br>
<br>
His granddaughters <br>
couldn’t wait to see him <br>
for the colourful dresses <br>
he had promised them, <br>
which they would wear the day of his return. <br>
Night fell upon him on the way <br>
his feet prayed to the weariness of wanderlust <br>
his eyelids hid the moon <br>
and he hung the key to the church on his chest. <br>
<br>
He dreamt he was a bell <br>
calling out for the disappeared <br>
and he tolled: ding, dang, dong-dong. <br>
He woke worrying <br>
for the morning was dressed in blood <br>
and the bodies <br>
that were left without offerings <br>
were thirsty. <br>
<br>
That’s how the people of Marutsíí lived, <br>
travelling the mountains, <br>
their dreams tucked in the sheath <br>
of their machetes. <br>
<br>
<br>

## Xì’ñú dón don <br>
<br>
Nikèè Xirágáá màrìkuro’ò <br>
dììn rí’na gájmàá ixí mí yaja, <br>
ikhaa nè màxnáxii <br>
ná gù’wá dxákuun idò mà’nè domingo, <br>
idò mà’thùún nimà rí màbìjii àkwin, <br>
rí mùni’ñáà adèè tsí nìkà <br>
ná Sierra màtangáà xuajñùn, <br>
khamí mùsngajmèè jàmbaà <br>
rí màwàtun idò màkanuù gù’wó. <br>
<br>
Kìra’tíìn ènè ijíín xìñíì, <br>
numuu rí nìwanúù rí ma’tsiì jnáwùn tsú’kwè, <br>
rí màjú’wun idò ma’khaà datìín.<br>
Nè’nè jína mú’ún ná jàmbaà, <br>
nì’nì tsákhun ná nìrigòò, <br>
nìkrá’wii gòn’ ná awuún itsúu iduu, <br>
khamí nistrákheè <br>
ñawún xkrugoò gù’wá dxákuun ná xoxtoò. <br>
<br>
Nigún’daa rí ajwàn’ <br>
rí nà’duù ná gù’wá dxákuun ñajùn, <br>
nàndxáwán èjèn tsí nigoó judiìn xàbò, <br>
ikànjgó nìgí’duù nì’duù: dín dan, dón don, <br>
idò nì’niì iduu, nìxnámineè gá’kho <br>
numuu rí phú nàgadíí i’dìún xàbò, <br>
ndiyeè ngamí numuu nimà tsí nàjndáwuún, <br>
tsí nangwá ni’ngòò muda’ rí gamaku. <br>
<br>
Xkwa’nii nijúwá <br>
mbòò Marutsíí nakí wajyúú mé’, <br>
nìriguùn ná júbàá, <br>
gájmàá xnu’daa rí giwàn’ jayuu ná xtoó chidìín. <br>
<br>
<br>

*Hubert Matiúwàa (trans Juana Adcock)*
